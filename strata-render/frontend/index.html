Startup Toolkit

Six tools. Sequenced in the order
you actually need them.

Most founders run them in the wrong order — building before validating, sizing before defining the customer, positioning before finding the JTBD. STRATA sequences them correctly so each one builds on the last.

Step 01 · Discovery
ICP Definition Engine
Define your Ideal Customer Profile with precision — firmographics, psychographics, pain intensity, buying context, and urgency score. The starting point every other framework depends on. Without a specific ICP, your PMF score is meaningless and your positioning is untestable.
ICP Score = Pain Intensity × Willingness to Pay × Accessibility · Min threshold to proceed: Pain ≥ 7/10
Step 02 · Motivation
Jobs-To-Be-Done (JTBD)
Map the job your customer hires your product to do — functional, emotional, and social progress. Clayton Christensen's most practical framework. Uncovers why customers switch products, what they're really buying, and what your real competitive set is. Not your product category. The job.
Job = Situation + Motivation + Expected Outcome · Hire events reveal your real switching triggers
Step 03 · Fit Score
PMF Signal Meter (Sean Ellis)
How many of your customers would be "very disappointed" if they could no longer use your product? 40%+ is the validated threshold for permission-to-scale. Below it: discover. Above it: build. The single most actionable metric for a pre-Series A company — but almost nobody measures it correctly.
PMF = % "very disappointed" ≥ 40% · Below 40%: discovery priority · 40–60%: scale permission · 60%+: dominant
Step 04 · Market Size
TAM / SAM / SOM Model
Both top-down and bottom-up market sizing. Know if you're chasing a $50M problem or a $5B one — before your next investor asks. Most founders confuse TAM (total theoretical market) with SOM (what you can realistically win in 3 years). They're not the same number and investors know the difference.
SOM = SAM × Realistic Win Rate × Timeline · Typical mistake: presenting TAM as the target
Step 05 · Adoption
Bass Diffusion Model
Model how your innovation will spread through the market using two parameters: p (innovation — people who buy without needing social proof) and q (imitation — people who buy because others did). High q = strong word-of-mouth engine. Low q = you'll need to spend more on acquisition forever. STRATA shows your curve live and tells you your chasm risk score.
N(t) = [p + q(N/M)] × (M − N) · Target: q ≥ 0.4 before scaling paid acquisition
Step 06 · Positioning
Lean Positioning Canvas
Before you write a single word of copy or hire a marketing person, you need one sentence that defines: for whom, against what alternative, and the single most important reason you win. Simple. Rigorous. Testable. If three different people on your team give three different answers, your positioning is broken — and so is your sales process.
For [ICP] who [pain], unlike [alternative], we [outcome] · Test: can everyone on your team say it identically?

CRIT_001 · Customer-Problem Mismatch
You may be solving a problem people have but not one they urgently want solved. There's a difference between "interesting" and "I need this fixed this week." Check: would 40%+ say "very disappointed" without you? Below threshold — every feature you build is money you spent on the wrong problem. Stop. Discover.
CRITICAL
CRIT_002 · Premature Scaling
Marketing or sales spend before your retention curves flatten and LTV:CAC exceeds 3:1 is the #1 cause of runway burn at the seed stage. You're not acquiring customers — you're paying to discover that the product still doesn't fit. Validate with 50+ customers, get 40%+ PMF, clear 3:1 LTV:CAC, then scale acquisition. In that order.
HIGH
WARN_003 · Vanity Feedback Loop
If more than 40% of your discovery interviews are with warm intros — friends, investors' portfolio companies, your own network — your PMF score is artificially inflated. Warm prospects tell you what you want to hear. Cold prospects tell you what's true. At least 60% of your discovery interviews must be with people who had no prior relationship with you or your product.
MEDIUM
INFO_004 · Bass Imitation Gap
Your current q coefficient (0.38) is below the SaaS category average (0.48 for PLG products, 0.55 for community-driven). Word-of-mouth is not naturally compounding at the rate you need before Series A. This means your CAC will rise as you exhaust organic channels. Engineer referral mechanics, community touchpoints, and "wow moments" to increase q before you scale acquisition.
